📓 Daily Journaling Prompts

  1. What emotion am I carrying most right now, and where do I feel it in my body?
  2. What is one thing I am grateful for today that I often overlook?
  3. What would I tell a close friend who was feeling the way I feel right now?
  4. What boundaries do I need to set or reinforce this week?
  5. What does my ideal day of rest look like?
  6. Who in my life makes me feel truly seen? How can I connect with them more?
  7. What fear is holding me back right now? Is it realistic?
  8. What is one small thing I can do today to care for my mental health?
  9. What am I avoiding that I know I need to face?
  10. What does growth look like for me right now?
  11. When did I last feel truly at peace? What was happening?
  12. What would I do today if I knew I could not fail?
  13. What drains my energy the most — and what gives it back?
  14. How have I grown in the last 6 months?
  15. What do I need to forgive myself for?

🧘 Self-Help Emotional Toolkit

Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

Inhale for 4 counts → Hold for 4 → Exhale for 4 → Hold for 4. Repeat 4 times. Reduces anxiety instantly.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. Brings you back to the present.

Body Scan

Close your eyes. Slowly move your awareness from your feet to your head, noticing tension and releasing it.

Gratitude Practice

Write down 3 specific things you are grateful for today. Do this every morning for 21 days for best results.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Tense each muscle group for 5 seconds then release. Start from your feet and work upward to your face.

🩺 Mental Health First Aid

Signs to watch for

Withdrawal from friends, sudden mood changes, talking about hopelessness, neglecting self-care, giving away possessions.

How to approach someone

Choose a quiet private moment. Say "I've noticed you seem down — I care about you. Would you like to talk?" Listen without judging.

What NOT to do

Don't minimise their feelings. Don't say "just cheer up." Don't leave someone in crisis alone.

When to escalate

If someone mentions suicide or self-harm, take it seriously. Stay with them and help connect them to a professional immediately.
